# Smart Water Level Controller using Verilog HDL

## Project Overview

The Smart Water Level Controller is a digital design project developed during the C2S (Chip to Startup) Training Program. The system is designed to monitor water levels in a storage tank and automatically control the water pump based on predefined level conditions.

The project demonstrates the practical application of Verilog HDL, RTL design concepts, digital logic implementation, and simulation-based verification used in modern FPGA and VLSI design workflows.

## Objectives

* Monitor water levels using digital input signals.
* Automate pump control to maintain an appropriate water level.
* Prevent water overflow and dry-run conditions.
* Understand RTL design and simulation methodologies.
* Gain hands-on experience with industry-standard EDA tools.

## Technologies and Concepts Used

* Verilog HDL
* RTL (Register Transfer Level) Design
* Digital Logic Design
* FPGA Design Concepts
* Simulation and Waveform Analysis
* EDA Tools

## System Functionality

The controller continuously monitors the status of:

* Low Water Level
* Medium Water Level
* High Water Level

Based on these inputs, the controller automatically performs the following actions:

* Turns the pump ON when the water level falls below the required threshold.
* Maintains normal operation during intermediate water levels.
* Turns the pump OFF when the tank reaches the maximum water level.
* Prevents unnecessary pump operation and water wastage.

## Simulation and Verification

The design was verified through simulation using EDA tools. Waveform analysis was performed to validate the behavior of the controller under different water level conditions.

The simulation results confirm the correct operation of:

* Water level detection logic
* Pump control logic
* State transitions
* Output response to varying input conditions
